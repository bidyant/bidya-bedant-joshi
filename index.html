Gangadhar Meher University, Sambalpur

2020 - 2023

I have a deep passion for Physics, and it led me to embark on a fulfilling 3-year journey as a BSc Physics student at GMU. Despite the challenges posed by COVID-19, our classes initially shifted online, but we later resumed offline classes and engaging lab work in the 2nd year. One thing that stands out about the School of Physics at GMU is the amazing support from the teachers. They consistently encourage students to explore productive endeavors and think critically. Their emphasis is on understanding the concepts rather than memorization.

During my 3-year BSc program, I delved into a wide array of fascinating subjects, including Mathematical techniques, Newtonian & classical dynamics, Electro dynamics, Special theory of relativity, Quantum mechanics, Nuclear & particle physics, Thermal physics & Statistical mechanics, Optics, analog & digital electronics, solid-state physics, and Nano Technology.

I was selected as class representative in school of physics GMU for the 3 years. It was challenging one, but I accepted it. I have a deep love for research, rather than just studing physics. I found immense joy in research, and as a result, I took the lead in organizing several science-based programs. Among these, I lead three project presentation programs within our department, showcasing innovative research by fellow students.

During my second year, I had a memorable experience with the Analog Electronics project under the guidence of Prof. SK Das in SCHOOL OF PHYSICS GMU. In a team effort, we worked on an Audio amplifier project using OP-AMP from scratch, managing to keep costs lower than industrial options by incorporating recycled materials.
After completing the audio amplifier project under Prof. Das's guidance, I worked on an edutech project called eGyanaVigyana. This project aims to provide a comprehensive preparation package for undergraduate students targeting exams like IIT JAM, JEST, TIFR. Using Android with Java, I developed the app, However, as my final semesters and PG entrance exams were approaching, there were a few finishing touches and content upload part left. With Prof. Das's support, we are working diligently to add content and questions to the app before its launch on the Play Store.
In my 5th semester, I with my project mates developed a low-cost, rechargeable battery operated semiconductor laser using waste products, as part of the solid-state and device chapter, and presented it during the project presentation session.
In my final semester, I undertook the independent design of an electronic circuit based on SSHI and SECI techniques to generate electricity from piezoelectric materials, utilizing stress or pressure. With the guidance of Dr. Pranti Purohit, I worked with PhD scholar Bomkesh sir to build and test the circuit in our GMU physics Lab. All the links and details regarding my project works during BSc are available on my LinkedIn profile.

Android App development

2022 - 2023

During my college days at GMU, my curiosity for different technologies was unstoppable. I devoted around 2-4 hours every midnight to learn and work on various projects after completing my study hours. Initially, I focused on React.js and Next.js, building web applications. However, while working on the eGyanaVigyana project, I became fascinated with Android app development.
During the lockdown days, I had already gained knowledge of Java, which made learning Android development a smooth journey for me. I began with the basics, focusing on creating projects using the MVC architecture. As I progressed, I delved into advanced concepts like Work Manager, services, and multi-threading, enabling me to develop complex and feature-rich Android applications with ease.
Diving into Android, I learned about the core concepts of the operating system, its life cycle, different architectures, real-time rendering, and Firebase integration. This deep exploration allowed me to create an edutech application for the School of Physics at GMU.

After completing my BSc degree, I spent a month in Sambalpur, preparing for the CUET exam and finishing my piezoelectric project. During that time, I learned Kotlin since Google was favoring it over Java for Android development. As I already knew Python and Java, I quickly grasped Kotlin and coroutines. I began developing Android apps in Kotlin, and after completing my project, I returned to my village. That's when I started working on my dream project, the 'Growth Tracker App'.
This app allows users to monitor plant growth and create captivating time-lapse videos. With a user-friendly interface and custom camera shutter button, users can capture images at their chosen time intervals, even when the app runs in the background. The app securely stores the images and compiles them into stunning time-lapse videos, making it a valuable tool for gardening enthusiasts, students, and nature lovers to document and visualize plant development. I created two versions using CameraX and Camera2 API.
During my free time, I continued learning Android by working on projects, and it has been an exciting journey of discovery and development.

Photography & Videography

2023 - present

My photography journey began with my smartphone, which had a main camera, ultra-wide camera, and macro camera that captured amazing shots. During my college days, I took some pictures, but after completing my BSc, I had free time, so I started learning photography from various sources.

I began by understanding basic camera settings like exposure triangle and how different lenses work. Then, I explored composition techniques like the rule of thirds and the golden ratio. As I mastered the pro mode of my smartphone, I learned to adjust aperture, shutter speed, ISO, and manual focus. I learned Lightroom for color correction and having prior knowledge of Photoshop, it was easier for me to grasp. I then dived into color grading in Premiere Pro, which opened up new possibilities for creating stunning short films. This way, I continued my journey of photography and videography, constantly exploring and improving my skills.

To enhance my photography, I invested in a camera lens package from Adcom Technology, which included macro, wide-angle, fish-eye, and telephoto lenses along with a tripod. With these gadgets, I started exploring photography and relaunched 'Bidyant Sir ki Classes' as 'Electromagnetic Bytes'. Now, in my free time, I thoroughly enjoy capturing the beauty of nature through my lens and store it in digital format bytes...
