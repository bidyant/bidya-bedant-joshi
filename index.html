Innovative Optical Instrument Design

During 2nd year of bachelor’s

In my second year of bachelor’s studies, I delved into solid-state physics, laser applications, and superconductivity, alongside wave optics. These topics introduced me to the fascinating phenomena demonstrating the wave properties of light, such as:

  • Young's Double-Slit Experiment
  • Light Interference
  • Fresnel and Fraunhofer Diffraction
  • Michelson Interferometer
Project: Cost-Effective Optical Bench with Laser Mount

Inspired by my studies and leveraging my knowledge of electronics, I designed and built an optical instrument tailored for demonstrating these optical phenomena effectively and affordably.

Key Features of the Instrument:
  • Construction:
    • Used a basic, standard, cheap metallic element as the optical bench.
    • Integrated a rechargeable, battery-operated semiconductor laser mounted on a sliding mechanism.
  • Adjustable Laser Mount: The laser mount was designed to slide smoothly and be positioned precisely along the optical bench, allowing for accurate experimental setups.
  • Cost Efficiency: The entire device was built for just INR 200, making it an accessible teaching aid for high schools.
  • Applications: The instrument could demonstrate phenomena like interference, diffraction, and the working of a Michelson interferometer. It was designed to be extendable and modifiable for further applications.

This project was a significant achievement, blending physics and electronics to create a practical teaching tool. By developing a cost-effective solution, I contributed to making advanced optical experiments more accessible for high school students, enhancing their understanding through hands-on visualization.
